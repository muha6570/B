Overview of the Battle

The Battle of Vimy Ridge was one of the most significant battles of World War I, involving Canadian forces who fought to capture the ridge from German troops in April 1917.

When and Where the Battle Occurred

The battle took place from April 9 to April 12, 1917, at Vimy Ridge in northern France.

Goals of the Battle

The goal was to seize Vimy Ridge, which had been held by German forces, to break through their defenses.

Who Was Involved

Primarily fought by Canadian forces against the German army.

Conditions and Experiences of Soldiers

The conditions at Vimy Ridge were harsh, with soldiers facing heavy artillery fire, trenches filled with mud, and poor weather conditions.

Weapons of Significance in the Battle

Several weapons were crucial, including the Lee-Enfield rifle, artillery, and machine guns.
